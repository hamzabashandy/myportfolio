The target of the studio was to design creative and conceptual soluations to existing problems in the surrounding urban space.

My deliverable was to create a new river bus stop instead of the existing one situated in Imbaba, a neighbourhood in northern Giza, Egypt, by investigating the current station issues. The use of basic shapes in the design was a necessity.
My aim was to create a diffrent experience for citizens instead of just going in or out of the bus stop.

Imbaba is a old neighbourhood that dated back to 1833 and by interviewing the original inhabitatants of the area, it showed that many ignore the history of their own place.

A | Bus Route

B | Form Generation

The Square is my base Form. The concept has been generating by bending the square twice at specific points with defined angles based the inclination of the cliff and to offer shelter from the sun.

C | Isometric

LCD screens fixed on the ground to tackle the ignorance issue of the place. The screen will display the area history the whole day so people waiting for their bus can benefit from it.
