Responsive wall is an installation that respond to people movement. It consists of a rectangular panel that can expand and contract on the principles of Origami. The panel repeats horizontally and vertically in order to create a screen that can take place in different places and in which artistic works can be presented.
| How does it work?
In state mode, panels are closed. Several motion sensors are placed inside the panels and send a signal to the microcontroller which rotate a servo motor attached to the panels. The panels expand and contract according to the distance between it and the person.

A | Thinking Process

Firstly, it was necessary to create the paper shape. Then analyze the moving points to recreate it on Grasshopper. Secondly, it was necessary to calculate the angle of rotation of each point. Since the base form is rectangular there were different angles. Then, 'Firefly' was used to connect the wall in the grasshopper with the 'PIR motion sensor' to test the contraction and expansion of the wall. The system is also connected to a "servomotor" which rotates with the same proportions of the panels. 'Remap' has been used several times to re-evaluate and redefine the numbers of each element.
