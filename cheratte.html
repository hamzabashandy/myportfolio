The primary goal of the project is Densification. Like a tightrope walker in search of his balance, the game consisted of densifying the premises, while preserving the existing: finding the right balance between an initial identity of places and the new, brought by our interventions; Make sure that the old and the new are in tune. Two main axes serve as a balance in the project:
1 | Densify without falling into the extreme, and keep the spirit of community while opening on the outside.
2 | Preserve the natural appropriation of the places by the inhabitants while inviting "the visitor" to stop and to take advantage of these same spaces.

A | ChERATTE, THE HISTORY

Inscribed in a recent history, but so particular in the evolution of cities and worker housing in Belgium, the old coal mine of Cheratte today presents a state of decay. Disused and emptied of most of its equipment, it is now the object of a partial demolition permit, in spite of a patrimonial quality and evident memory stakes. On a wider scale, the site of the former coal mining includes a housing project designed on the model of the garden cities in 1920. Historically linked to the activity of coal mining, this city today shows a singular face, resulting from the many socio-economic and demographic evolutions of recent decades. It is precisely this housing project that formed the study area of the workshop this year. It was approached in its relation with the city of Visé and the nearby urban tissue, as well as in a perspective concerned with valuing the memory of the place as a whole

B | THE EPICENTRE

Looking at the urban fabric, we find that the city of cherate is isolated by strong edge: the river, highway and the railway. our goal was to dissolve these edges by creating different activities and places around cheratte. The coal mine will be transformed into a cultural place hosting various events.
R5: A new centre will be created between the coal mine and the city of cheratte connected by urban elevators. The centre will include shopping centre, offices and a university campus to study agriculture.
R1: Aiming to encourage citizens to produce their own food, agricultural plots along the railway will be created. These plots will be used by the habitants of the city and also by the students to develop research and expriments.
